The Dominating Order in Graph Reals

This page introduces and analyzes the Dominating Order on the field of Graph Reals \( \widehat{\mathcal{Q}_G} \). Defined via positivity of associated operators, this canonical partial order encodes a powerful hierarchy among graph-limit elements, unifying the behavior of positive graph invariants, spectral dominance, and homomorphism densities.

1. Definition via Operator Positivity

Given \( x, y \in \widehat{\mathcal{Q}_G} \), we write: \[ x \preceq_{\text{dom}} y \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \Psi(y - x) \succeq 0 \] where \( \Psi \colon \widehat{\mathcal{Q}_G} \to \mathcal{B}(L^2) \) is the regularized operator embedding. This means that \( y \) dominates \( x \) whenever their difference maps to a positive semi-definite operator.

The set of all elements dominating \( 0 \), denoted \( \mathcal{C} = \{ x : \Psi(x) \succeq 0 \} \), is the positive cone of the Graph Reals.

2. Properties of the Dominating Order

  • Partial, not total: \( \preceq_{\text{dom}} \) is a partial order; many elements cannot be compared directly.
  • Refinement of invariant order: If \( x \preceq_{\text{dom}} y \), then \( v(x) \le v(y) \), \( e(x) \le e(y) \), and \( t(F, x) \le t(F, y) \) for all test graphs \( F \).
  • Archimedean: If \( n x \preceq_{\text{dom}} y \) for all \( n \in \mathbb{N} \), then \( x \preceq_{\text{dom}} 0 \). This excludes infinitesimal positive elements.
  • Closedness: The cone \( \mathcal{C} \) is closed under the pair-operator norm metric.

3. Graph-Theoretic Interpretation

The relation \( x \preceq_{\text{dom}} y \) asserts that every positive graph parameter increases from \( x \) to \( y \). This includes:

  • Vertex and edge count;
  • Homomorphism densities \( t(F, x) \);
  • Spectral invariants of the kernel \( \Psi(x) \);
  • Linear evaluations via any positive state \( \phi(\Psi(\cdot)) \).

Thus, the Dominating Order captures structural containment, density, and richness in a single framework.

4. Functional Structure and the Order-Unit Norm

The Graph Reals become an Archimedean order-unit space with unit element \( 1 \in \widehat{\mathcal{Q}_G} \). We define: \[ \|x\|_u := \inf\{ \lambda > 0 : -\lambda \cdot 1 \preceq_{\text{dom}} x \preceq_{\text{dom}} \lambda \cdot 1 \}. \] This norm is equivalent to the pair-operator norm, and it allows the application of Banach lattice techniques, convex analysis, and Choquet theory to study the geometry of \( \mathcal{C} \).

5. Compatibility with the Pair Metric

The Dominating Order is strongly compatible with the metric structure of \( \widehat{\mathcal{Q}_G} \):

  • Closed cone: \( \mathcal{C} \) is closed under the pair metric.
  • Order intervals: Sets of the form \( \{ z : x \preceq_{\text{dom}} z \preceq_{\text{dom}} y \} \) are compact and convex in the metric topology.
  • Monotonicity: If \( 0 \preceq_{\text{dom}} x \preceq_{\text{dom}} y \), then \( d(0,x) \le d(0,y) \).

6. Future Directions

The Dominating Order opens new research directions across graph theory, analysis, and convex geometry:

  • Classifying extremal rays of \( \mathcal{C} \) to identify irreducible graph limits;
  • Studying states and dual functionals to define new monotone invariants;
  • Applying functional calculus to define operator means and interpolate between graph structures;
  • Exploring projective metrics and contraction mappings for iterative graph processes.
