Ghost Tracking Cosmology – Initial Pass Constraints

This page summarizes a first-pass synthesis of current constraints on the ghost tracking cosmology model. The goal is not a full likelihood analysis, but a qualitative map of which regions of parameter space remain viable once three tests are applied:

  • the early-universe test (CMB and BBN),
  • the background expansion test (SN, BAO, CMB distances), and
  • the growth test (redshift-space distortions and weak lensing).
Parameter recap. The model uses the parameters \[ \Omega_r,\quad \Omega_m,\quad \Omega_g,\quad f_{\text{trk}},\quad w_{g,\infty}, \] with flatness \(\Omega_r + \Omega_m + \Omega_g = 1\). The ghost density evolves as \[ \rho_g(a) = \rho_{g0}\left[f_{\text{trk}}\,a^{-3} + (1-f_{\text{trk}})a^{-B}\right], \quad B = 3\bigl(1 + w_{g,\infty}\bigr), \] so that ghost energy tracks matter at early times and behaves like dark energy at late times.

1. Snapshot of Test Outcomes

Table 1 summarizes how each test currently acts on the ghost sector, in the sense of a qualitative “pass with constraints” versus clear exclusion or data-poor uncertainty.

Table 1. Qualitative outcome of the three tests for the ghost tracking model.
Test Main observables Effective constraint Qualitative outcome
Early-universe test CMB anisotropies, CMB lensing, BBN-inferred baryon density. Fixes the combination \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) to the Planck-level matter density, and restricts the early tracking contribution \(f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) to a small fraction of the total energy density at recombination. Passes, with upper bounds on \(f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\).
Background expansion test Type Ia SNe distances, BAO scale, and distance to last scattering. Drives the Hubble history \[ \frac{H^2(a)}{H_0^2} = \Omega_r a^{-4} + (\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g)a^{-3} + \Omega_g(1-f_{\text{trk}})a^{-B} \] extremely close to the best-fit \(\Lambda\)CDM expansion history, keeping \(w_{g,\infty}\) near \(-1\) and limiting large deviations. Passes, with the ghost sector confined to a narrow neighbourhood of the \(\Lambda\)CDM expansion history.
Growth test Redshift-space distortions \(f\sigma_8(z)\), galaxy clustering, weak lensing (\(S_8\)). Favors a growth history with slightly suppressed clustering relative to Planck \(\Lambda\)CDM, guiding \(\Omega_m\) toward values that can be slightly lower, while \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) and \(H(a)\) stay close to the background fit. Passes, with growth data further trimming the allowed region in \((\Omega_g, f_{\text{trk}}, w_{g,\infty})\)-space.

In all three cases, current observations leave viable parameter space. The tests act together as a set of strong priors shaping the ghost sector rather than eliminating it.

2. Early-Universe Test – CMB and BBN

In the early universe the ghost sector contributes an additional dustlike piece, \[ \rho_g(a) \approx \rho_{g0} f_{\text{trk}} a^{-3}, \] which combines with ordinary matter to form an effective dust density \[ \rho_{\text{m,eff}}(a) \approx \bigl(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\bigr)\rho_{\text{crit},0}\,a^{-3}. \]

CMB and BBN measurements fix the physical matter density \(\omega_m = \Omega_{\text{m,eff}} h^2\) at roughly the percent level. This pins \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) to the Planck value and restricts the size of \(f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) to the regime where the ghost contribution behaves like a modest “early dark matter” component rather than a large extra dust reservoir.

Outcome. Early-universe data already require \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) to match the Planck-inferred value and place a clear upper bound on \(f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\). The ghost sector still has room at the level of a few percent of the energy density at recombination, so the early-universe test alone does not remove the model.

3. Background Expansion Test – Distances

The same combination \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) enters the background expansion together with the late-time ghost mode: \[ \frac{H^2(a)}{H_0^2} = \Omega_r a^{-4} + \bigl(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\bigr)a^{-3} + \Omega_g(1-f_{\text{trk}})a^{-B}. \]

Type Ia supernovae, BAO, and the CMB distance to last scattering already prefer an expansion history extremely close to that of flat \(\Lambda\)CDM. Because the ghost sector produces a smooth two-parameter family of \(w_g(a)\), and includes \(\Lambda\)CDM as a special case, this test compresses \((\Omega_g, f_{\text{trk}}, w_{g,\infty})\) into a narrow region where \(H(a)\) essentially mirrors the \(\Lambda\)CDM best fit.

Outcome. Distance data encourage \(w_{g,\infty}\) values close to \(-1\) and limit large tracking fractions, yet still admit a continuous band of ghost-tracking models that reproduce the preferred \(\Lambda\)CDM-like expansion history.

4. Growth Test – Structure Formation

Linear growth responds both to the effective matter content and to the expansion rate. In the tracking model, the growth rate \(f\sigma_8(z)\) depends on

  • the early-time effective matter fraction \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\), and
  • the detailed expansion history controlled by \(\Omega_g, f_{\text{trk}}, w_{g,\infty}\).

Redshift-space distortions and weak lensing measurements currently lean toward a growth amplitude slightly lower than that implied by Planck \(\Lambda\)CDM extrapolated to late times. Within the tracking framework this behaviour is captured by choosing \(\Omega_m\) a bit lower while holding \(\Omega_m + f_{\text{trk}}\Omega_g\) and \(H(a)\) near the background best fit, something the ghost sector accomplishes naturally.

Outcome. Growth data carve additional structure in the allowed region of \((\Omega_g, f_{\text{trk}}, w_{g,\infty})\), favouring combinations that gently suppress growth relative to a Planck-only \(\Lambda\)CDM fit. Within current uncertainties the minimal ghost-tracking model remains compatible with these measurements.

5. Interpretation and Next Steps

The three tests together give a coherent first picture:

  • The ghost sector behaves as a tightly regulated companion to standard matter and dark energy rather than a free-form additive component.
  • Early-universe and distance data strongly shape the model yet still admit a continuous family of solutions with background expansion very close to the Planck \(\Lambda\)CDM best fit.
  • Growth data encourage a mild shift in the balance between \(\Omega_m\) and the ghost contribution, something the tracking law provides in a controlled way.

A full parameter scan with a Boltzmann code and global likelihood analysis would convert this qualitative map into quantitative bounds on \(\Omega_g, f_{\text{trk}}, w_{g,\infty}\). The present initial pass already indicates that the model clears the main observational hurdles and earns a more detailed numerical treatment.
