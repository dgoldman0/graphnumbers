Ghost Dark Energy from Pregeometric Ghost Edges

This page sketches a minimal cosmological model in which late–time cosmic acceleration arises from ghost edges, relational degrees of freedom in a graph-valued pregeometric algebra (Graph Reals). Ordinary geometry corresponds to the image of an operator map \( T \), while ghost edges live in \( \ker T \) and represent pure relations without geometric location. Coarse-graining the ghost sector yields a homogeneous fluid with an equation-of-state parameter \( w_g \), producing a ghost dark energy component in a flat FRW universe.

1. Toy Algebra and Ghost Edges

We work in a simple real vector space \[ \mathcal{A}_{\text{toy}} = \mathrm{span}_\mathbb{R}\{ K_1,\, \mathbb{I}_\circ,\, \mathbb{E}_\varnothing \}, \] where:

  • \( K_1 \): a single-vertex graph (one “unit” of location).
  • \( \mathbb{I}_\circ \): a “unit cycle” element (one vertex, one edge, zero net tension).
  • \( \mathbb{E}_\varnothing \): the ghost edge, defined by \[ \mathbb{E}_\varnothing = \mathbb{I}_\circ - K_1. \] It has one unit of relation, zero vertices, and sits in the kernel of the operator map.

A linear operator map \( T \) sends these generators to bounded operators (or scalars in the toy reduction), \[ T(K_1) = 1, \quad T(\mathbb{I}_\circ) = 1, \quad T(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = 0, \] so \( \mathbb{E}_\varnothing \in \ker T \).

Any element of the toy algebra can be written as \[ H_{\text{toy}} = \alpha K_1 + \beta \mathbb{I}_\circ + \gamma \mathbb{E}_\varnothing. \] The coefficient \( \alpha + \beta \) controls the “visible” geometric contribution through \( T(H_{\text{toy}}) \), while \( \gamma \) measures the ghost content, invisible to \( T \) but available to other observables.

Definition (Ghost Edge Content). For a large pregeometric configuration modeled in Graph Reals, the ghost edge content is the effective coefficient \( \gamma \) of the ghost edge basis element \( \mathbb{E}_\varnothing \) in the corresponding toy reduction. This content lives in the kernel of the operator map and represents pure relation without geometric localization.

2. Coarse-Graining to a Cosmic Ghost Fluid

Consider a huge comoving region in the universe and average the ghost coefficient over that region:

  • Let \( \bar{\gamma}(t) \) denote the coarse-grained ghost edge content per unit comoving volume at cosmological time \( t \).

We define the ghost energy density by \[ \rho_g(t) = \Lambda_g \, \bar{\gamma}(t), \] where \( \Lambda_g \) is a constant with dimensions of energy density, encoding the conversion from microscopic ghost content to macroscopic energy.

At homogeneous background level, \( \bar{\gamma}(t) \) depends only on \( t \), so the ghost sector behaves as a spatially uniform fluid. To obtain the simplest working theory, we assign a constant equation of state

\[ p_g = w_g\, \rho_g, \] where \( w_g \) is an effective macroscopic parameter describing how ghost energy responds to expansion.

3. FRW Cosmology with Ghost Dark Energy

In a spatially flat FRW universe with scale factor \( a(t) \), the total energy density includes radiation, matter, and the ghost component: \[ \rho_{\text{tot}}(t) = \rho_r(t) + \rho_m(t) + \rho_g(t). \]

Energy conservation for the ghost fluid is \[ \frac{d\rho_g}{dt} + 3H(1 + w_g)\rho_g = 0, \] where \( H = \dot{a}/a \) is the Hubble parameter. For constant \( w_g \), this integrates to \[ \rho_g(a) = \rho_{g0}\, a^{-3(1 + w_g)}, \] with \( \rho_{g0} \) the present-day ghost energy density.

Introducing the usual density parameters \[ \Omega_i = \frac{\rho_{i0}}{\rho_{\text{crit},0}}, \quad \rho_{\text{crit},0} = \frac{3H_0^2}{8\pi G}, \] and assuming exact spatial flatness, \[ \Omega_r + \Omega_m + \Omega_g = 1, \] the Friedmann equation becomes \[ \frac{H^2(a)}{H_0^2} = \Omega_r a^{-4} + \Omega_m a^{-3} + \Omega_g a^{-3(1 + w_g)}. \]

In terms of redshift \( z \) with \( a = 1/(1+z) \), this is \[ \frac{H^2(z)}{H_0^2} = \Omega_r (1+z)^4 + \Omega_m (1+z)^3 + \Omega_g (1+z)^{3(1 + w_g)}. \]

At background level this matches the standard constant-\( w \) dark energy form. The novelty lies in the pregeometric interpretation of \( \Omega_g \) and \( w_g \).

4. Interpretation of \(\Omega_g\) and \(w_g\)

  • Ghost density fraction. The parameter \( \Omega_g \) is the present-day fractional ghost energy density, \[ \Omega_g = \frac{\rho_{g0}}{\rho_{\text{crit},0}} = \frac{\Lambda_g \bar{\gamma}_0}{\rho_{\text{crit},0}}. \] It measures how much ghost edge content the universe currently carries when viewed through the Graph Reals framework.
  • Ghost equation of state. The parameter \( w_g \) encodes the macroscopic response of the ghost sector to expansion:
    • \( w_g \approx -1 \): ghost content per physical volume remains roughly steady, mimicking a cosmological constant.
    • \( w_g > -1 \): ghost energy dilutes faster; ghost edges gradually lose macroscopic influence.
    • \( w_g < -1 \): ghost energy grows relative to comoving volume, potentially leading to a phantom-like future.

In a fuller Graph Reals model, \( w_g \) would be derived from the dynamics of ghost edges: how \( \bar{\gamma} \) changes when volumes rescale, and how interactions in the kernel sector redistribute ghost content. The constant-\( w_g \) case here serves as a minimal testing parameterization.

5. Phenomenology and Outlook

At the level of homogeneous cosmology, the model predicts:

  • A modified Hubble history \( H(z) \) determined by \( (\Omega_g, w_g) \) and the usual radiation and matter densities.
  • Corresponding changes to luminosity distances for supernovae and angular-diameter distances for BAO and CMB.
  • A distinct cosmic age and deceleration history depending on the sign and magnitude of \( 1 + w_g \).

Observational constraints on \( \Omega_g \) and \( w_g \) thus translate into bounds on the amount and behavior of ghost edge content in the underlying pregeometry. Deviations from \( w_g = -1 \) can be read as evidence for nontrivial ghost dynamics, such as phase transitions, condensation, or coupling between the kernel and image sectors of Graph Reals.

Extensions of this working theory include:

  • Allowing a time-dependent \( w_g(z) \) tied to specific ghost-edge processes (e.g. geometrogenesis).
  • Studying perturbations in the ghost sector and their impact on the growth of large-scale structure.
  • Relating ghost dark energy more explicitly to the graph-based operator completion and the structure of \( \ker T \) in the full Graph Reals framework.

Even in this stripped-down form, ghost dark energy provides a concrete bridge between graph-based pregeometry and cosmological data: a way to interpret the dark sector as the large-scale shadow of relational degrees of freedom that ordinary geometric operators fail to see.
