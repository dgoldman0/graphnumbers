Ordered Graph Reals: Directed Graph Arithmetic and Operator Fields

This page introduces the Ordered Graph Reals: a field-like extension of finite directed graphs into an analytic continuum, based on weighted step kernels and operator norms. Building on the Graph Reals framework, this variant encodes edge directionality intrinsically through asymmetry in operator kernels, enabling a generalization to non-symmetric graph limits.

1. Directed Graph Algebra

  • Directed Graph Naturals \( \mathcal{N}_G^{\rightarrow} \): Simple finite directed graphs (no loops), with disjoint union \( \sqcup \) and Cartesian product \( \Box \), forming a commutative semiring.
  • Directed Graph Integers \( \mathcal{Z}_G^{\rightarrow} \): Formal differences of directed graphs, forming a ring.
  • Directed Graph Rationals \( \mathcal{Q}_G^{\rightarrow} \): Field of fractions \( x/y \) where \( y \ne 0 \), equipped with the usual addition and multiplication of fractions.

2. Weighted Operator Embedding

Each directed graph is embedded into \( L^2([0,1]) \) via a refined partition and a distinct edge weighting. The associated step-kernel \[ W_G(x,y) = w(i,j) \quad \text{if } x \in B_i,\ y \in B_j,\ \text{and } i \to j \text{ in } G \] defines a bounded operator \( T_G \) on \( L^2 \) by integration. The choice of weights ensures injectivity and analytic invertibility for all nonzero graphs.

3. Operator Metric and Completion

Define a metric on directed Graph Rationals by \[ d(x/y, x'/y') = \left\| T_x T_y^{-1} - T_{x'} T_{y'}^{-1} \right\|_{2 \to 2}. \] This operator-norm metric turns the image of \( \mathcal{Q}_G^{\rightarrow} \) into a metric space. Completion under this metric yields the Ordered Graph Reals, a complete topological field.

4. Continuity and Field Structure

Addition, multiplication, and inversion are continuous under the operator norm. All constructions are defined purely via bounded linear maps, allowing a singular-value calculus to replace self-adjoint spectral theory.

5. Compatibility and Embedding of Undirected Theory

The traditional Graph Reals embed isometrically into the Ordered Graph Reals by symmetrizing graphs and weights. That is, any undirected graph \( G \) can be viewed as a symmetric directed graph with \( i \to j \) and \( j \to i \) whenever \( \{i,j\} \in E(G) \), and \( w(i,j) = w(j,i) \). The corresponding operator \( T_G \) then matches the undirected step-kernel operator.

7. Status and Open Questions

This theory is provisional but well-grounded. Major theoretical gaps from the undirected case have been repaired by the introduction of weighted kernels and directional partitions. However, formal proofs of Archimedean ordering, spectral continuity, and compatibility with known graph limit objects remain open and in progress.
