Ghost Edge in Graph Reals: Construction, Counts, and the Ghost Line

This page gives a careful construction of the ghost edge inside the Graph Reals \( \mathbb{R}_G \), using the Graph–Field Metric and its completion. The goals are:

  • Define vertex and edge count maps \( v,e \) on graph naturals, integers, and rationals.
  • Show that \( v \) and \( e \) extend to continuous maps on \( \mathbb{R}_G \).
  • Construct the ghost edge \( \mathbb{E}_\varnothing \) as a Graph Real and verify \( v(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing)=0 \), \( e(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing)=1 \).
  • Introduce the ghost line generated by \( \mathbb{E}_\varnothing \) and describe its basic geometric and physical interpretation.

1. From Graph Naturals to Graph Rationals

Graph naturals. Let \( \mathcal{N}_G \) be the commutative monoid of isomorphism classes of finite graphs under disjoint union \( \sqcup \), with identity the empty graph \( \varnothing \). For a finite graph \( G \), write \( [G] \in \mathcal{N}_G \) for its class.

Define the vertex count and edge count on graph naturals by \[ v_{\mathcal{N}}([G]) := |V(G)|, \qquad e_{\mathcal{N}}([G]) := |E(G)|. \] These are additive monoid homomorphisms \[ v_{\mathcal{N}}, e_{\mathcal{N}} : (\mathcal{N}_G, \sqcup) \longrightarrow (\mathbb{Z}, +), \] since \( v_{\mathcal{N}}([G \sqcup H]) = v_{\mathcal{N}}([G]) + v_{\mathcal{N}}([H]) \) and similarly for \( e_{\mathcal{N}} \).

Graph integers. The graph integers \( \mathcal{Z}_G \) form the Grothendieck group of \( (\mathcal{N}_G, \sqcup) \). Elements are equivalence classes of pairs \((A,B)\) of graph naturals, written formally as \( A-B \), modulo the relation \[ (A,B) \sim (A',B') \quad\text{if}\quad \exists C\in\mathcal{N}_G\ \text{with}\ A \sqcup B' \sqcup C \cong A' \sqcup B \sqcup C. \] The class of \( (A,B) \) is denoted \( [A]-[B] \in \mathcal{Z}_G \).
Lemma 1 (Counts extend to graph integers). There exist unique group homomorphisms \[ v_{\mathcal{Z}}, e_{\mathcal{Z}} : \mathcal{Z}_G \to \mathbb{Z} \] such that \[ v_{\mathcal{Z}}([G]-[H]) = v_{\mathcal{N}}([G]) - v_{\mathcal{N}}([H]), \qquad e_{\mathcal{Z}}([G]-[H]) = e_{\mathcal{N}}([G]) - e_{\mathcal{N}}([H]). \]

This is the standard universal property of the Grothendieck group: any additive map out of \( \mathcal{N}_G \) extends uniquely to an additive map out of \( \mathcal{Z}_G \).

Graph rationals. The graph rationals \( \mathcal{Q}_G \) are the field of fractions of \( \mathcal{Z}_G \). As a \( \mathbb{Q} \)-vector space, every element of \( \mathcal{Q}_G \) can be written as a finite sum \[ q = \sum_{i=1}^k a_i x_i, \qquad a_i \in \mathbb{Q},\ x_i \in \mathcal{Z}_G. \]
Lemma 2 (Counts extend to graph rationals). There exist unique \( \mathbb{Q} \)-linear maps \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}, e_{\mathcal{Q}} : \mathcal{Q}_G \to \mathbb{R} \] such that, on \( \mathcal{Z}_G \subset \mathcal{Q}_G \), \( v_{\mathcal{Q}}|_{\mathcal{Z}_G} = v_{\mathcal{Z}} \) and \( e_{\mathcal{Q}}|_{\mathcal{Z}_G} = e_{\mathcal{Z}}. \) Concretely, for \( q = \sum_i a_i x_i \), \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q) = \sum_i a_i\,v_{\mathcal{Z}}(x_i), \qquad e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q) = \sum_i a_i\,e_{\mathcal{Z}}(x_i). \]

All constructions used below live inside the \( \mathbb{Q} \)-span of actual finite graphs, so the values of \( v_{\mathcal{Q}} \) and \( e_{\mathcal{Q}} \) always reduce to explicit expressions in counts of vertices and edges for concrete graphs.

2. Operator Embedding and the Graph–Field Metric

The Graph–Field Metric is defined via a scaled operator embedding \[ \Psi : \mathcal{Q}_G \longrightarrow \mathcal{B}(H), \] where \( H \) is a suitable Hilbert space (for example \( H=L^2([0,1]) \) when using graphon kernels). For \( q\in\mathcal{Q}_G \), define the operator pair \[ G(q) = \begin{cases} \bigl(\Psi(q),\; f(\|\Psi(q)\|)\,\Psi(q)^{-1}\bigr), & q\neq 0,\\[0.5em] (0,0), & q = 0, \end{cases} \] where \( f \) is a continuous damping function chosen so that \( f(\|\Psi(q)\|)\to 0 \) as \( \|\Psi(q)\|\to 0 \).

Equip \( \mathcal{B}(H)\times\mathcal{B}(H) \) with the product norm \[ \|(A,B)\|_{\times} = \|A\|_{\mathrm{op}} + \|B\|_{\mathrm{op}}. \] The Graph–Field Metric on \( \mathcal{Q}_G \) is then \[ d_G(q,r) := \bigl\| G(q) - G(r) \bigr\|_{\times}. \] Its completion is denoted \( \mathbb{R}_G \), and elements of \( \mathbb{R}_G \) are Graph Reals.

Graph Reals. The metric completion of \( (\mathcal{Q}_G, d_G) \) is \[ \mathbb{R}_G := \widehat{\mathcal{Q}_G}^{\ d_G}. \] A point \( x \in \mathbb{R}_G \) is an equivalence class of Cauchy sequences \( (q_n) \) in \( \mathcal{Q}_G \), where two sequences \( (q_n) \) and \( (r_n) \) are equivalent if \( d_G(q_n, r_n) \to 0 \).

3. Making Counts Lipschitz (and Therefore Continuous)

To interpret vertex and edge counts on Graph Reals, \( v_{\mathcal{Q}} \) and \( e_{\mathcal{Q}} \) should behave well with respect to the metric \( d_G \). The strategy is:

  1. Realize \( v_{\mathcal{Q}} \) and \( e_{\mathcal{Q}} \) as bounded linear functionals of the operator embedding \( \Psi \).
  2. Use a general lemma: every Lipschitz map on a metric space extends uniquely and continuously to its completion.
Lemma 3 (Counts as operator functionals). There exist bounded linear functionals \[ \Phi_v, \Phi_e : \mathcal{B}(H) \to \mathbb{R} \] such that, for all graph rationals \( q \in \mathcal{Q}_G \), \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q) = \Phi_v(\Psi(q)), \qquad e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q) = \Phi_e(\Psi(q)). \]

Sketch of construction: on the subspace of \( \mathcal{Q}_G \) spanned by graph integers, the map \( q \mapsto \Psi(q) \) has image inside a linear subspace of \( \mathcal{B}(H) \). On that subspace, define linear functionals \( \widetilde{v}, \widetilde{e} \) by \[ \widetilde{v}(\Psi(q)) := v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q),\qquad \widetilde{e}(\Psi(q)) := e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q). \] These are well defined because \( \Psi \) is linear. Using Hahn–Banach, extend \( \widetilde{v}, \widetilde{e} \) to bounded linear functionals \( \Phi_v, \Phi_e \) on all of \( \mathcal{B}(H) \).

From this, for any \( q,r \in \mathcal{Q}_G \), \[ |v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q) - v_{\mathcal{Q}}(r)| = \bigl|\Phi_v(\Psi(q) - \Psi(r))\bigr| \le \|\Phi_v\|\,\|\Psi(q) - \Psi(r)\|_{\mathrm{op}} \le \|\Phi_v\|\,d_G(q,r). \] The same reasoning yields \[ |e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q) - e_{\mathcal{Q}}(r)| \le \|\Phi_e\|\,d_G(q,r). \]

Corollary 4 (Lipschitz counts on graph rationals). The maps \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}, e_{\mathcal{Q}} : (\mathcal{Q}_G, d_G) \to \mathbb{R} \] are Lipschitz, hence continuous.
Lemma 5 (Lipschitz maps extend to the completion). Let \( (X,d) \) be a metric space and \( \widehat{X} \) its completion. If \( F:X\to\mathbb{R} \) is \( L \)-Lipschitz, then there exists a unique continuous map \[ \widehat{F} : \widehat{X} \to \mathbb{R} \] such that \( \widehat{F}|_X = F \). For any Cauchy sequence \( (x_n) \) in \( X \) representing a point \( x \in \widehat{X} \), \[ \widehat{F}(x) = \lim_{n\to\infty} F(x_n). \]

Applying this lemma to \( X = \mathcal{Q}_G \), \( d = d_G \) and \( F = v_{\mathcal{Q}}, e_{\mathcal{Q}} \) yields continuous extensions.

Corollary 6 (Counts on Graph Reals). There exist unique continuous maps \[ v, e : \mathbb{R}_G \to \mathbb{R} \] such that, for any Graph Real \( x = [q_n] \in \mathbb{R}_G \), \[ v(x) = \lim_{n\to\infty} v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q_n), \qquad e(x) = \lim_{n\to\infty} e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q_n). \] On the dense subspace \( \mathcal{Q}_G \subset \mathbb{R}_G \), these coincide with \( v_{\mathcal{Q}} \) and \( e_{\mathcal{Q}} \).

4. The Unit Cycle and the Ghost Edge

The ghost edge arises from two complementary limiting procedures: regularized cycles and regularized complete graphs.

Unit cycle. For each \( n\ge 3 \), let \( C_n \) be the cycle graph on \( n \) vertices. Define \[ q_n := \frac{1}{n}\,C_n \in \mathcal{Q}_G. \] The sequence \( (q_n)_{n\ge 3} \) is Cauchy in \( (\mathcal{Q}_G,d_G) \), and its equivalence class in \( \mathbb{R}_G \) is the unit cycle \[ \mathbb{I}_\circ := [q_n] \in \mathbb{R}_G. \]

On each \( C_n \), \( v_{\mathcal{N}}([C_n]) = n \) and \( e_{\mathcal{N}}([C_n]) = n. \) By \( \mathbb{Q} \)-linearity, \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q_n) = v_{\mathcal{Q}}\Bigl(\frac{1}{n}C_n\Bigr) = \frac{1}{n}\,v_{\mathcal{N}}([C_n]) = 1, \] \[ e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q_n) = e_{\mathcal{Q}}\Bigl(\frac{1}{n}C_n\Bigr) = \frac{1}{n}\,e_{\mathcal{N}}([C_n]) = 1. \] Hence \[ v(\mathbb{I}_\circ) = \lim_{n\to\infty} v_{\mathcal{Q}}(q_n) = 1, \qquad e(\mathbb{I}_\circ) = \lim_{n\to\infty} e_{\mathcal{Q}}(q_n) = 1. \]

Ghost edge via cycle subtraction. Let \( K_1 \) be the single-vertex graph with no edges. Define \[ r_n := \frac{1}{n}\,C_n - K_1 \in \mathcal{Q}_G. \] The sequence \( (r_n)_{n\ge 3} \) is Cauchy in \( (\mathcal{Q}_G,d_G) \), and its equivalence class in \( \mathbb{R}_G \) is the ghost edge \[ \mathbb{E}_\varnothing := [r_n] = \mathbb{I}_\circ - K_1 \in \mathbb{R}_G. \]

For each \( n \), \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}(r_n) = v_{\mathcal{Q}}\Bigl(\frac{1}{n}C_n\Bigr) - v_{\mathcal{Q}}(K_1) = 1 - 1 = 0, \] and \[ e_{\mathcal{Q}}(r_n) = e_{\mathcal{Q}}\Bigl(\frac{1}{n}C_n\Bigr) - e_{\mathcal{Q}}(K_1) = 1 - 0 = 1. \] Therefore the constant sequences \( v_{\mathcal{Q}}(r_n) \equiv 0 \) and \( e_{\mathcal{Q}}(r_n) \equiv 1 \) converge, and by definition of the extensions \( v,e \), \[ v(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = \lim_{n\to\infty} v_{\mathcal{Q}}(r_n) = 0, \qquad e(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = \lim_{n\to\infty} e_{\mathcal{Q}}(r_n) = 1. \] The ghost edge has one unit of edge content and zero vertices.

Ghost edge via complete graphs. For each \( n\ge 2 \), let \( K_n \) be the complete graph on \( n \) vertices. Define \[ s_n := \frac{2}{n(n-1)}\,K_n \in \mathcal{Q}_G. \] The corresponding equivalence class in \( \mathbb{R}_G \) is again the ghost edge: \[ \mathbb{E}_\varnothing = [s_n]. \]

For each \( n \), \( v_{\mathcal{N}}([K_n]) = n \) and \( e_{\mathcal{N}}([K_n]) = \frac{n(n-1)}{2} \), so \[ v_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_n) = \frac{2}{n(n-1)}\,v_{\mathcal{N}}([K_n]) = \frac{2}{n(n-1)}\,n = \frac{2}{n-1} \longrightarrow 0, \] \[ e_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_n) = \frac{2}{n(n-1)}\,e_{\mathcal{N}}([K_n]) = \frac{2}{n(n-1)} \cdot \frac{n(n-1)}{2} = 1. \] Thus the limits are again \[ v(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = 0, \qquad e(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = 1, \] consistent with the cycle-based definition. The ghost edge is independent of the chosen representing sequence in \( \mathcal{Q}_G \).

5. The Ghost Line and a Two-Plane of Real + Ghost

Ghost line. In the Graph Reals, the ghost line is \[ L_{\mathrm{ghost}} := \bigl\{ a\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing : a \in \mathbb{R} \bigr\} \subset \mathbb{R}_G. \] The map \[ \phi_{\mathrm{ghost}} : \mathbb{R} \longrightarrow \mathbb{R}_G,\quad a \longmapsto a\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing \] is a continuous group homomorphism (for addition), since multiplication in \( \mathbb{R}_G \) is continuous in the Graph–Field Metric.

Along this line, the counts scale linearly: \[ v(a\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = a\,v(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = 0, \qquad e(a\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = a\,e(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = a, \] at least for rational \( a \) by construction, and by continuity for all real \( a \) obtained as limits. Points on \( L_{\mathrm{ghost}} \) carry pure edge content with no vertex mass.

Real slice plus ghost line. The embedding of ordinary reals into Graph Reals is denoted \[ \kappa : \mathbb{R} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{R}_G. \] Consider the subset \[ P := \bigl\{\kappa(r) + s\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing : r,s \in \mathbb{R}\bigr\} \subset \mathbb{R}_G. \] Elements of \( P \) can be viewed as having coordinates \( (r,s) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \) in a two-dimensional plane spanned by the real scalar direction and the ghost direction.

For \( x = \kappa(r) + s\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing \in P \), the counts decompose as \[ v(x) = v(\kappa(r)) + v(s\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = v(\kappa(r)), \] \[ e(x) = e(\kappa(r)) + e(s\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) = e(\kappa(r)) + s. \] In this sense:

  • \( r \) parametrizes the scalar/metric-visible part of the Graph Real.
  • \( s \) parametrizes the ghost or vertexless relational content.
Ghost amplitude and distance from geometry. On the plane \( P \), the coordinate \( s \) can be interpreted as a ghost amplitude: \[ \mathrm{GhostAmp}(\kappa(r) + s\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing) := s. \] More generally, for any Graph Real \( x \in \mathbb{R}_G \), one can define a distance to the Real slice \[ \mathrm{GeomDev}(x) := \inf_{r\in\mathbb{R}} d_G\bigl(x,\kappa(r)\bigr), \] which measures how far \( x \) sits from purely scalar geometry in the Graph–Field Metric. Ghost-rich configurations have nonzero ghost amplitude and typically lie at positive distance from the Real slice.

6. Summary

The Graph–Field Metric and its completion \( \mathbb{R}_G \) support a precise treatment of the ghost edge:

  • Vertex and edge counts extend from finite graphs through graph integers \( \mathcal{Z}_G \) and graph rationals \( \mathcal{Q}_G \) to continuous maps \( v,e : \mathbb{R}_G \to \mathbb{R} \).
  • The unit cycle \( \mathbb{I}_\circ \) is a Graph Real with \( v(\mathbb{I}_\circ)=1 \), \( e(\mathbb{I}_\circ)=1 \).
  • The ghost edge \( \mathbb{E}_\varnothing = \mathbb{I}_\circ - K_1 \) admits consistent Cauchy representatives and satisfies \( v(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing)=0 \), \( e(\mathbb{E}_\varnothing)=1 \).
  • The ghost line \( L_{\mathrm{ghost}} = \{a\,\mathbb{E}_\varnothing\} \) gives a one-dimensional family of vertexless edge configurations inside \( \mathbb{R}_G \).
  • Together with the embedded real line, the ghost direction spans a two-dimensional plane of Graph Reals where one coordinate captures scalar geometry and the other captures residual relational structure.
